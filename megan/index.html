Sun in Leo

The Sun provides us with our central vital source of energy. It represents the conscious self-hood of the individual psyche – the power to do and to be. It functions as the cloak our soul has chosen to creatively actualize its purpose, and the heart and core of beingness. This urge represented by the Sun is the desire for full self-expression, for manifesting personal power and talent in ways that inspire others to express their creatively. This is sometimes referred to as our yang energy, and it is traditionally where we shine.

The Sun rules Leo, and there is no other sign in which the solar light can shine quite as naturally. No heart glows harder or brighter than this. Leo energy is regal and dignified, proudly and grandly assuming dominion over the marvelous magical creativity of life. With a birthright to glory and unique personal access to inner child, the generous lion rules over the heart. Because of this, the Sun in Leo ferociously defends the innocent and the vulnerable – any sort of injustice or betrayal is a disgrace that has no place in its kingdom.

In the 7th House, the Leo Sun can feel that its light only truly shines when it shares with others the self-worth and substantiality of identity it came into the world with. It strives to radiate a basis for existence and to illuminate the path of selfhood for others – to reveal to others the own light within them by sharing its own heavenly light. The overwhelming need to be recognized and adored for its creative gifts and undeniable warmth can result in vicious self-criticism if others fail to provide it with the affection and reassurance it needs. Yet when provided with a deserved doting partner who sees the value of its compassion, the 7th House Leo Sun's inner glow is restored and recharged.

You put others center stage by encouraging them, creating warmth and sunshine to gain the admiration and attention you deserve. By acknowledging others' uniqeueness, you are able to honor your own childlike innocence and spontaneous vitality instead of acting in needlessly dramatic or understated or overstated ways. You gain vitality when involved in all manners of partnerships.

Your soul has chosen the right to:

  • To express yourself gloriously.
  • To be noticed and admired just for being yourself.
  • To be acknowledged by others as an equal.
  • To be treated as someone special.

Moon in Leo

The Moon acts as a reflector of our true selves (Sun) and explains how we experience ourselves on a less conscious level. Typically the governing influence during childhood, the Moon manifests as emotions, instincts, intuition, self-care, and self-discovery. The Moon is what we need for emotional satisfaction and also the process through which we may separate ourselves emotionally from other people, where issues of personal insecurity may arise to be purified. This is sometimes referred to as our yin energy.

With the moon in Leo, emotions are a relentless roller coaster of highs and lows. Rain and sunshine at the same time – an undeniable energetic force that commands the stage. Everything is felt tenfold through the spotlight lens of the heart, so dramatic performances and fiery rampages become natural responses to demand attention toward unmet needs. The lunar Moon in the solar light of Leo can seem like a never-ending magic trick – the aching desire to manifest miracles seemingly out of thin air, the promise of the audience's applause driving you. The real magic lies in the energy you can create from your own inner applause once you acknowledge that your personal magic is best channeled when you boldly support yourself.

In the 7th House, the Leo Moon can feel as if it is not allowed to admit it is struggling until it all becomes too much. The sting of abandonment and betrayal has conditioned the Leo Moon to meet the emotional needs of loved ones while rejecting its own, and then exploding at others or withdrawing completing when they criticize you for not doing enough. The approval and praise from others becomes like a drug you have always needed and cannot survive without. As others draw away, the 7th House Leo Moon pours more of itself into them, promising its entire kingdom and pleading "Please don't go."

You can create the self-worth you need by noticing that achieving admiration from others does not necessarily result in success. The values projected may not be what others want or need. The key is to relinquish the position of director in your life drama and focus on others' needs in a way that frees you to express your flamboyant, generous nature. By uplifting others, you find enjoyment and happiness and realize the fun and loyalty that surrounds you.

In past lives: You were celebrated royalty in other lives: actors, actresses, musicians, kings, queens, and stars in one form or another. Consequently, you came into this lifetime with a need for recognition, approval, and praise. You are accustomed to applause, and insecurity results when you feel ignored or not treated as someone special.

In this lifetime: You are learning to dedicate yourself to more universal causes and to be a vehicle for energy that promotes the goals of humanity. When you are able to see yourself as a channel for helping your community, you can accept your childlike qualities and forgive yourself for any mistakes because you are secure in knowing you had the highest motive. Clarity about the ideals of humanity empowers you to offer approrval and vital support to others without expectations of loyalty, opening the gateway to a flood of unexpected appreciation of your generosity of spirit when you least expect it.

Capricorn Rising

Also called the Ascendant (AC) for the sign that was rising on the Eastern horizon specific to the time and location of birth. Abstractly, these are soul desires on a subjective level which operate as the window or lens through which we see the world and the instinctive mask we present as to the outside. It is the filter through which everything we are internally is projected into the external world and through which everything in our outer environment is personally interpreted by us. This is the lens through which we filter our perspective on life and also how others first see us or respond to us – how we begin/start things. The AC also represents the legacy available to us and the innate qualities that have been the most fully developed and mastered through numerous past life experiences – our inherited gifts.

Ruled by restrictive Saturn, Capricorn energy chisels its paternal signature into the foundation of each building it constructs. It strives for a personal standard of excellence and grinds away with its eyes focused on the long-game, knowing that hard work is non-negotiable under the inescapable law of cause and effect. The mature authoritarian writes the unyielding laws of time and space itself, declaring the security in boundaries and growing convinced that only wilderness lies beyond its walls — but a heart guarded can be as lonely as a heart destroyed. This energy can feel subconsciously restricted no matter the effort it puts in. The overwhelming reponsibility the outside world demands is a heavy weight to bear – a crushing weight that screams it will never be perfect or solid enough for the recognition and stability it seeks.

Mercury in Virgo

Mercury signifies our urge to express ourselves verbally from the analytical left-side of the brain. Mercury defines the nature of our mental talents and intellectual approach but also the process by which we may create endless mental worries and negative expectations that lead to isolating and withholding ourselves from true contact with others out of fear. This planet correlates to the human need to give order and linear structure to inherently phenomenal reality and is the function by which we collect data or organize the thoughts ignited by our internal and external environments.

Mercury rules Virgo, so it is able to work its magic especially well here. This is the placement of the ultimate alchemist. The asleep fear of Merucy in Virgo is indulging in the tendency to analyze and judge what is right and wrong in yourself and others – creating endless categories in your mind. This makes it difficult to communicate in an orderly and confident way, and the self-criticism can be suffocating.

When you are able to trust the universal, unseen order of things, you stop feeling defensive about how actions reflect imperfection. This allows you to develop self-tolerance; you are then empowered to accomplish any job with a sense of perfection but without applying the same high standards of performance to your personal life. By detaching from expecting perfection in your relationships and communications with others, you can be transformed to a new level.

In the 8th house, Mercury gives you a special ability to perceive the motives of others that are hidden in their communication. You speak in ways that utterly transform other people, for good or for ill. The Mercury in the 8th mind operates like a psychic spy, covertly ripping apart the layers of everything while revealing just enough to forge deep connections with others. Even if you seems disinterested or distracted, the unconscious is scrutinising information at the speed of x-ray light.
